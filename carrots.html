What bed is suitable?

Carrots fit beds in a sunny and windswept garden area. In the shade, near bushes and trees, carrots accumulate less carotene. In addition, on a shady bed expanse for a carrot fly.

What soil is suitable for growing carrots?

Only finely cloddy soil is suitable for growing carrots, that is, light and medium loamy, sandy loam, peat, cultivated sod-podzolic. On stony, heavy clay and dense soils, carrots will grow low-juicy and ugly.

Do I need to dig a bed for carrots?

A bed for carrots should be dug in the fall, preferably without a layer turnover. Since the length of root crops in many varieties reaches 30 cm, it is necessary to dig on a full bayonet of a shovel. In the spring, the soil should not be dug up. Break the lumps with a hoe, and then carefully level with a rake, having previously applied 1 sq. meter for 1 teaspoon of urea. A week before sowing seeds, the bed is abundantly watered with warm water. Then it is covered with plastic wrap to prevent drying and better heating.
